MUSEUMS INFORMATION

- The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization. Construction began in 2002 near the Giza Pyramids and it opened in phases between 2023 and 2024. Covering over 480,000 square meters, it houses more than 100,000 artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection displayed together for the first time. It also features one of the world’s largest conservation centers and a modern design inspired by pyramid geometry, offering an advanced interactive visitor experience

- The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC), opened in 2021 in historic Fustat, presents the development of Egyptian civilization from prehistoric times to the modern era. It includes the Royal Mummies Hall, featuring great kings like Ramses II, whose mummies were transferred in a globally celebrated parade in 2021. The museum also includes interactive galleries and research facilities, offering a comprehensive view of Egypt’s history.

- Abdeen Palace

Abdeen Palace is one of Egypt’s most important historic palaces. Construction began in 1863 during the reign of Khedive Ismail to serve as the seat of government instead of the Citadel. It became one of the most luxurious royal palaces in the world, known for its European-style architecture and rich decorations. Today, it houses several museums including the Arms Museum and Royal Collections Museum, reflecting Egypt’s royal history and major political events of the 19th and early 20th centuries.

- The Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art in Cairo, opened in 1903, is one of the largest and most important Islamic art museums in the world. It houses over 100,000 artifacts from across the Islamic world, including rare manuscripts, woodwork, textiles, metalwork, and glass objects. It showcases the development of Islamic art over centuries and features traditional Islamic architectural design, with recent restoration efforts after damage in past years.

- The Coptic Museum

The Coptic Museum, founded in 1908 in Old Cairo by Marcus Simaika Pasha, houses the largest collection of Coptic artifacts in the world, including icons, manuscripts, textiles, and carved wood. It reflects the development of Christian art in Egypt and is located near historic churches, forming part of an important religious and historical complex.

- The Alexandria National Museum

The Alexandria National Museum, opened in 2003 inside an Italian-style palace, displays over 1,800 artifacts representing the various civilizations that shaped Alexandria, including Pharaonic, Greek, Roman, and Islamic periods. It highlights the city’s historical role as a major cultural center and is organized into floors by historical eras.

- Baron Empain Palace

The Baron Empain Palace, built in 1911 by Belgian industrialist Édouard Empain, founder of Heliopolis, is one of Egypt’s most unique architectural landmarks. It features an unusual design inspired by Indian and Asian architecture, with intricate carvings and statues. The palace became famous for various legends and stories surrounding it, and it has recently been restored and reopened as a museum showcasing the history and development of Heliopolis, representing a unique blend of cultural and architectural influences.
